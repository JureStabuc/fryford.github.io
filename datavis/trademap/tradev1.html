The UK exported £350 billion of goods in 2013, (up 16 % on 2012, but mostly due to erratic trade in Non-monetary Gold). The UK imported £420 billion of goods in 2013 (down 5% on 2012).

In 2013 the largest UK export commodities were Non-monetary Gold (£51 billion) and Petroleum, petroleum products & related materials (£36 billion). The largest UK import commodities in 2013 were Petroleum, petroleum products & related materials (£43 billion) and Road vehicles (including air cushion vehicles) (£42 billion)

Germany

In 2013 the UK’s biggest trading partner when it comes to goods was Germany, with bilateral trade (exports plus imports) of £87 billion. Goods exports to Germany in 2013 were £31 billion.

The top exports were Petroleum, petroleum products & related materials (£3.9 billion), and Road vehicles (including air cushion vehicles) (£2.5 billion). Goods imports from Germany in 2013 were £56 billion. The top imports were Road vehicles (including air cushion vehicles) (£16.7 billion), and Medicinal & pharmaceutical products (£3.8 billion).

Trade partners - Exports

The UKs largest goods export destination in 2013 was Switzerland, with exports of £45 billion. However 88% of this was in Non-monetary Gold, an erratic commodity. In 2012 the Switzerland was the UK’s 7th largest goods export destination. The top goods export destinations for 2013 were:

    Switzerland (£45bn)
    United States (£40bn)
    Germany (£31bn)
    Netherlands (£24bn)
    France (£22bn)
